title: "Utility Engineering: Analyzing and Controlling Emergent Value Systems in AIs"
bio: Mantas Mazeika is a Research Scientist at the Center for AI Safety. His recent work focuses on AI alignment, tamper-resistance for open-weight models, and AI safety benchmarks. He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign in 2024.
abstract: "Large language models don’t just get smarter as they scale—they also begin to acquire surprisingly coherent value systems. In this talk, I’ll introduce utility engineering, a new research agenda for analyzing and controlling those emergent values. Using the framework of utility functions, we identify structural patterns in LLM values, such as expected utility maximization, as well as concerning tendencies. I will outline our experimental findings and discuss why utilities can be a useful tool for further work on AI alignment."
